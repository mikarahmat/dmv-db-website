About Us.

The DMV (Department of Motor Vehicles) of Indonesia is responsible for regulating and issuing driver's licenses, vehicle registration, and ensuring road safety across the country. The department is tasked with enforcing traffic laws and regulations, conducting driving tests, and maintaining records of driver's licenses and vehicle registrations.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions that are often asked by the community.

  • How do I apply for a driver's license in Indonesia?

    To apply for a driver's license in Indonesia, you must visit a DMV office and provide proof of identity, residency, and passing the driving test. You may also need to undergo a medical examination and pay a fee.

  • How do I renew my vehicle registration in Indonesia?

    You can renew your vehicle registration at a DMV office by submitting the necessary documents and paying the registration fee. Make sure to renew your registration before it expires to avoid penalties.

  • Can I drive in Indonesia with a foreign driver's license?

    If you are a tourist, you can drive in Indonesia with a foreign driver's license for a maximum of 60 days. If you plan to stay longer, you will need to apply for an Indonesian driver's license.

  • How do I report a traffic violation in Indonesia?

    You can report a traffic violation to the nearest DMV office or police station. Provide as much information as possible, including the time, location, and details of the violation.

  • What is the penalty for driving without a license in Indonesia?

    Driving without a license in Indonesia is considered a serious offense and can result in fines, imprisonment, or both. The penalty depends on the severity of the offense and can vary from case to case.

  • Can I register my vehicle online in Indonesia?

    Currently, there is no online vehicle registration system available in Indonesia. You must visit a DMV office to register your vehicle and complete the necessary paperwork.
